# Sales-Performance-Dashboard
This is a multi-country Sales Analysis Dashboard built in Power BI, analysing retail sales performance across six countries — Canada, China, India, Nigeria, the UK, and the US. It covers $4.14M in total sales, 3,000 total orders, $959.29K in total profit, and $76.55K in total discounts across seven product categories. The dashboard is fully interactive with slicers for Country, Store Location, Category, and Payment Method, making it a versatile tool for regional sales managers, category heads, and finance teams.

## Oberservation 
Monthly sales are highly inconsistent throughout the year: The monthly trend line swings sharply, from $0.44M in January down to $0.26M in July and August, then recovering to a year-high $0.52M in December. The mid-year trough (June–August) represents a significant seasonal dip that the business needs to address.

Tuesday sales ($0.29M) are 62% lower than Sunday ($0.77M): The daily trend bar chart reveals an extreme spread between Sunday (peak) and Tuesday (trough). Friday and Saturday also record low figures ($0.31M and $0.65M), while Monday ($0.75M) is the second strongest day, suggesting a consumer-driven weekend/start-of-week purchasing pattern.

All seven categories are remarkably close in revenue: Home & Kitchen leads at $0.73M, and the lowest categories are at $0.67M — a spread of only $0.06M across seven categories. This near-equal distribution suggests the business has a broadly diversified product portfolio with no single category dominating or collapsing.

All three payment methods are almost perfectly equal: Mobile Payment (34.25%), Cash (33.84%), and Credit Card (32.71%) are split within a 1.54 percentage point range. This is an exceptional balance, suggesting either a deliberate payment infrastructure investment or a very diverse global customer base with different payment preferences averaging out.

London and Birmingham show high discount AND high profit: In the scatter plot, London and Birmingham (UK) sit in the upper-right quadrant — receiving among the highest discounts while also generating among the highest profits. This counterintuitive pattern suggests discounting in these cities is driving volume without eroding margins, possibly through a premium product mix.

New York shows high profit but relatively modest discounts: New York (US) appears on the far right of the scatter plot with a high profit level but at a higher discount range (~$65K). This suggests the US market may be using deeper discounting to achieve its profit levels compared to UK cities, which achieve similar results with less discounting.

## Conclusion
The Sales Dashboard presents a picture of a broadly healthy but operationally uneven global retail business. With $4.14M in sales, a 23.2% profit margin, and near-perfect payment method diversification, the business has strong commercial foundations across six international markets. However, significant volatility emerges on closer inspection: the monthly trend swings dramatically from a January high of $0.44M to a mid-year low of $0.26M in July and August before recovering to a December peak of $0.52M. The daily sales pattern is even more striking — Tuesday generates barely a quarter of Sunday's revenue, revealing a severe weekday commercial gap. Category performance is encouragingly balanced, with all seven product lines within $0.06M of each other, reducing concentration risk. The UK cities of London and Birmingham demonstrate an interesting discount-efficiency model, achieving high profits even with elevated discounting, while Nigeria's cities operate at a smaller scale but form a meaningful part of the global footprint. The business's biggest structural challenge is smoothing its mid-year and mid-week troughs without sacrificing the margin quality evidenced in the current profit figures.
